Continuous Casting in Steel Production

Continuous casting is a key process in modern steel production. Molten steel is solidified into slabs, blooms or billets while being guided through multiple segments equipped with rolls.

A single continuous casting machine may include hundreds or even thousands of rolls operating under extreme thermal and mechanical stresses.

The Challenge of Managing Casting Rolls

Continuous casting rolls are exposed to severe industrial conditions including high temperatures, water spray cooling, mechanical loads and corrosive environments.

Steel plants must manage large fleets of rolls distributed across multiple casting lines. Rolls can be located in different stages of their lifecycle:

  • Installed on the casting machine
  • Stored in maintenance workshops
  • Awaiting refurbishment
  • Being reconditioned
  • Scrapped

Industrial Roll Lifecycle Management

Efficient management of casting rolls requires reliable identification and lifecycle monitoring systems capable of tracking each component throughout its operational life.

Digital roll traceability improves maintenance planning, operational reliability and overall performance of continuous casting machines.
