TRACKROLL-ID®

Industrial RFID traceability system designed for the identification and lifecycle tracking of continuous casting rolls in steel plants.

Continuous Casting Process

Continuous casting is a key steelmaking process that allows the continuous production of semi-finished steel products such as slabs, billets and blooms. Molten steel is transferred from the ladle to the tundish and then into the mold where the initial solidification begins.

The strand is guided through a series of segments equipped with rollers until full solidification before cutting.

A typical continuous casting machine contains between 9 and 15 segments and can include more than 1000 rolls per casting line.

Challenges of Roll Management

Continuous casting rolls operate in extremely severe industrial conditions.

  • Constant exposure to very high temperatures
  • Mechanical stresses depending on their position in the casting line
  • Thermal fatigue and corrosive environments

Rollers may be at different stages of their lifecycle:

  • Installed on the casting machine
  • Stored in maintenance workshops
  • Awaiting refurbishment
  • Being reconditioned
  • Scrapped

Managing this large fleet of rollers is a major challenge for steel plants in terms of equipment availability, maintenance optimization and cost control.

TRACKROLL-ID® Concept

TRACKROLL-ID® is a complete industrial traceability solution for continuous casting rolls. It combines robust RFID identification with mobile data acquisition and centralized data management.

The system is based on five complementary components:

  • TRACKROLL-ID® Tag – RFID tag designed for harsh industrial environments
  • TRACKROLL-ID® Cross – Identification tag for roll support beams
  • TRACKROLL-ID® PDA – Portable industrial reader for field operations
  • TRACKROLL-ID® WiewApp – Mobile application for roll identification and field data entry
  • TRACKROLL-ID® myWiew – Web application for monitoring and data analysis

Field Integration

RFID TRACKROLL-ID®Tag are installed in dedicated housings machined into the roll structure according to a controlled installation procedure.

Each part of the equipment can be identified:

  • Rollers
  • Roll support beams
  • Casting cage

For example, a casting cage composed of 10 beams and 30 rollers incorporates more than 40 TRACKROLL-ID® Tag enabling precise cross-tracking of the equipment lifecycle.

Data Management and Software Platform

Data collected in the field using the portable reader TRACKROLL-ID® PDA is synchronized with the TRACKROLL-ID®WiewApp software platform.

The system provides secure access to operational data through role-based authentication and user management TRACKROLL-ID®myWiew.

  • Multi-criteria search and consultation
  • Equipment assembly and disassembly tracking
  • Inventory management
  • Roll pairing optimization
  • Movement tracking and maintenance history
  • Performance dashboards

Industrial Benefits

  • Digitalization of roll identification
  • Precise location of rollers
  • Complete lifecycle traceability
  • Roll lifespan analysis
  • Improved maintenance planning
  • Integration with ERP systems
  • Better operational visibility
