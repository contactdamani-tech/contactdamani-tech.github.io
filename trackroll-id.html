TRACKROLL-ID®

Industrial RFID traceability system designed for the identification and lifecycle tracking of continuous casting rolls in steel plants.

Continuous Casting Process

Continuous casting is a key steelmaking process that allows the continuous production of semi-finished steel products such as slabs, billets and blooms. Molten steel is transferred from the ladle to the tundish and then into the mold where the initial solidification begins. The strand is then guided through a series of segments equipped with rollers until full solidification before cutting.

A typical continuous casting machine contains between 9 and 15 segments and can include more than 1000 rolls per casting line.

Challenges of Roll Management

Continuous casting rolls operate in extremely severe industrial conditions:

  • Constant exposure to very high temperatures
  • Mechanical stress depending on their position in the casting line
  • Thermal fatigue and corrosive environments

Rollers can be at different stages of their lifecycle:

  • Installed on the casting machine
  • Stored in maintenance workshops
  • Awaiting refurbishment
  • Being reconditioned
  • Scrapped

Managing this large fleet of rollers is a major challenge for steel plants in terms of availability, maintenance optimization and cost control.

TRACKROLL-ID® Concept

TRACKROLL-ID® is a complete industrial traceability solution for continuous casting rolls. It combines robust RFID identification with mobile data acquisition and centralized data management.

The system is based on five complementary components:

  • TRACKROLL-ID®Tag – Robust RFID tags designed to withstand thermal and mechanical stresses
  • TRACKROLL-ID®Cross – Identification tags for roll support beams
  • TRACKROLL-ID®Pda – Portable industrial reader for field operations
  • TRACKROLL-ID®ViewApp – Mobile application for roll identification and field data entry
  • TRACKROLL-ID®myView – Web application for monitoring and data analysis

Field Integration

RFID tags are installed in dedicated housings machined directly into the roll structure according to a controlled installation procedure.

Each part of the equipment can be identified:

  • Rollers
  • Roll support beams
  • Segments

For example, a casting cage composed of 10 beams and 30 rollers includes more than 40 identification tags enabling precise cross-tracking of the equipment lifecycle.

Data Management and Software Platform

Data collected in the field using the portable reader is synchronized with the TRACKROLL-ID® software platform.

The system provides secure access to operational data through role-based authentication and user management.

Key features include:
  • Multi-criteria search and consultation
  • Equipment assembly and disassembly tracking
  • Inventory management
  • Roll pairing optimization
  • Movement tracking and maintenance history
  • Performance dashboards

Industrial Benefits

  • Complete lifecycle traceability
  • Roll lifespan analysis
  • Improved maintenance planning
  • Integration with ERP systems
  • Better operational visibility
