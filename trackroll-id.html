TRACKROLL-ID®

Industrial RFID traceability system designed for the identification and lifecycle tracking of continuous casting rolls in steel plants.

Download Technical Presentation (PDF)

Continuous Casting Process

Continuous casting is a key steelmaking process that allows the continuous production of semi-finished steel products such as slabs, billets and blooms. Molten steel is transferred from the ladle to the tundish and then into the mold where the initial solidification begins.

A typical continuous casting machine contains between 9 and 15 segments and can include more than 1000 rolls per casting line.

Challenges of Roll Management

  • Constant exposure to very high temperatures
  • Mechanical stress depending on their position in the casting line
  • Thermal fatigue and corrosive environments

Managing this large fleet of rollers is a major challenge for steel plants in terms of availability, maintenance optimization and cost control.

TRACKROLL-ID® Concept

  • TRACKROLL-ID® Tag – RFID tag for harsh industrial environments
  • TRACKROLL-ID® Cross – Identification tag for roll support beams
  • TRACKROLL-ID® PDA – Portable industrial reader
  • TRACKROLL-ID® ViewApp – Mobile application for field data collection
  • TRACKROLL-ID® myView – Web application for monitoring and analysis

Field Integration

RFID tags are installed in dedicated housings machined into the roll structure according to a controlled installation procedure.

  • Rollers
  • Roll support beams
  • Segments

Data Management

  • Assembly and disassembly tracking
  • Inventory management
  • Roll pairing optimization
  • Movement tracking and maintenance history

Industrial Benefits
